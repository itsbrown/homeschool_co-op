The Story of American Seekers Academy
In the shadow of 2020's unprecedented school closures, a visionary in New York recognized a profound gap in education: the need for a resilient, decentralized system that could never be shuttered. This system would welcome all children, irrespective of vaccination status, and instill core values like reading, writing, liberty, capitalism, and American exceptionalism—protected by armed guards safeguarding our nation's future. To navigate New York's regulatory hurdles, American Seekers Academy Ministries was established as a Private Membership Association, leveraging ministerial and PMA protections for operational freedom.
Drawing inspiration from Hillsdale College's Barney Charter School Initiative, ASA adopted premier curricula, including Hillsdale's Literacy Essentials program grounded in the Orton-Gillingham method, boasting near-perfect success rates in literacy. Complemented by authentic history, civics, and self-governance emphases, ASA aimed to recruit exceptional educators passionate about teaching. Breaking from traditional homeschool co-op norms, the academy extended sessions to three days a week and introduced a dress code, delivering a structured, school-like experience for families.
Amid the challenges of establishing a foothold in New York, ASA faced initial setbacks with three denials for operational approval. However, perseverance paid off in a pivotal milestone: securing permission to launch at the current Brighton location, marking the academy's first physical home and a testament to its resilient vision.
Amid the regulatory triumphs, ASA secured approval in June 2023 to commence operations at the Brighton location. Yet, the journey hit an early hurdle: low signups prevented a fall launch under the original model, forcing a tough decision to absorb the loss and pivot. This restructuring refined the academy's approach, aligning it with the current framework to better serve homeschool families, culminating in a renewed push for a January 2024 opening.
In the face of early adversities, ASA's doors swung open in January 2024, welcoming 16 children into a nurturing environment split between two classrooms: the spirited Yankee Doodles for kindergarteners and the inquisitive Seekers for grades 3-5. Yet, the launch was not without trials—a misalignment with the initial Seekers mentor led to her departure on day two. Rising to the occasion, the founders embodied their mission: the wife assumed the bulk of daily instruction, while the husband dove into teaching Literacy Essentials, ensuring the vision of liberty-infused, classical education endured. This dedication propelled the academy through its inaugural 10-week winter and spring sessions, laying a foundation of resilience and excellence.
By Fall 2024, ASA's impact had blossomed, with a full complement of Macaronis (ages 6 months to 3 years), 15 Yankee Doodles, 8 Tycoons (grades 1-2), 12 Seekers, and 2 Pioneers—reflecting a growing community drawn to its unique blend of structure, values, and flexibility.
As ASA matured, it embraced the full spectrum of classical education by introducing specialized programs for older students. The Pioneers, catering to grades 6-8, and the Patriots for grades 9-12, immerse learners in profound explorations of founding principles. Through studies of Aristotle's ethics, the American Founding, grammar, rhetoric, Socratic discussions, entrepreneurship, financial literacy, and self-governance, these young minds are equipped to become thoughtful, self-reliant leaders in a free society.
As ASA's roots deepened in the Fall 2024 session, a transformative shift occurred mid-term with the arrival of two exceptional educators. A natural-born teacher joined the team, swiftly assuming leadership of her class and infusing it with passion and expertise that aligned seamlessly with the academy's mission. Concurrently, a seasoned professor from a nearby university stepped in to guide the Pioneers and Patriots, leveraging his college-level experience to enrich discussions on Aristotle's ethics, the American Founding, and beyond—fostering a deeper appreciation for self-governance and entrepreneurial spirit among the older students.
These key additions liberated the founders from daily classroom responsibilities, enabling the visionary leader to channel energies into refining curricula, optimizing schedules, and expanding outreach efforts. Buoyed by the Fall session's resounding success, ASA launched its Winter 2025 session in January with this strengthened faculty lineup, including the two new experienced teachers and a dedicated Macaroni lead whose enthusiasm and innate leadership shone brightly in nurturing the youngest learners, proving her versatility in supporting the academy's holistic needs.
As ASA's momentum built through Winter 2025, the academy navigated evolving internal challenges with the same tenacity that defined its origins. With a stable team of experienced teachers and the eager Macaroni lead in place, attention turned to optimizing schedules, enriching content, and incorporating new extended days for extracurricular classes—opportunities designed to spark creativity and practical skills alongside classical foundations. However, striking the right balance between thrilling, hands-on projects and essential teachings presented hurdles, leading to the strategic integration of vetted external lesson plans and a specialized art curriculum. These adaptations ensured the program remained dynamic, values-driven, and true to its mission of fostering liberty-minded, self-governing young Americans.
This period of refinement fueled unprecedented expansion. By Fall 2025, ASA had scaled to two thriving locations, realizing its decentralized model on a broader scale. The bustling Brighton flagship overflowed with vitality, serving 65 children under the guidance of 5 skilled Mentors, 3 supportive aides, 1 dedicated coordinator, and 2 vigilant armed guards—creating a secure haven where classical education met real-world resilience. In parallel, the newly launched Greece site extended ASA's reach, nurturing 23 children with 4 mentors and 1 aide, drawing more families into a community committed to exceptionalism, capitalism, and unyielding access to learning.
In Winter 2025, ASA's innovative spirit shone through as it thoughtfully curated external resources to enhance its curriculum while staying true to its classical roots and values of liberty, capitalism, and self-governance. For art, the academy sourced comprehensive curricula from Teachers Pay Teachers, focusing on classical artists from the Renaissance and beyond—units that introduced students to masters like Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Rembrandt through hands-on projects and historical context, reinforcing the timeless beauty of Western heritage and encouraging creative expression as a form of individual freedom. Complementing this, drawing techniques were drawn from the "Learn How To Draw Hub" YouTube channel, offering step-by-step tutorials that empowered children with practical skills, fostering self-reliance and the joy of personal achievement in a way that echoed ASA's emphasis on entrepreneurial mindset.
For financial literacy, a 10-week lesson plan was integrated from readily available educational platforms (such as those from InCharge Debt Solutions or the Council for Economic Education), structured around core concepts like budgeting, saving, and investing. This was paired seamlessly with "CASHFLOW for Kids," the engaging board game created by Robert Kiyosaki—author of Rich Dad Poor Dad—which simulates real-world financial decisions in a fun, low-stakes environment, teaching children the principles of assets versus liabilities, cash flow management, and wealth-building. These selections aligned perfectly with ASA's mission: the art resources upheld the classical model's appreciation for enduring truths and beauty, while the financial elements directly instilled capitalist values, encouraging young minds to embrace economic independence, risk-taking, and American exceptionalism as pathways to liberty and self-governance.
The Fall 2025 expansions in Brighton and Greece not only scaled ASA's physical presence but also amplified its transformative impact on young lives, yielding remarkable student achievements that underscored the efficacy of its Orton-Gillingham-rooted Literacy Essentials program and values-driven approach. As of October 30, 2025, the academy boasted a 100% literacy proficiency rate among all returning students—a testament to the program's near-guaranteed success in building confident, capable readers. Even more inspiring, several students experienced dramatic leaps, advancing two full grade levels in reading within just two 10-week sessions, empowering them to engage more deeply with classical texts, historical narratives, and entrepreneurial concepts that form the bedrock of ASA's curriculum.
